4.6 Reflection on Goals & Motivation
Definition
Reflection on Goals & Motivation explores how individuals set objectives, sustain drive, and evaluate whether their goals align with personal values. It emphasizes understanding intrinsic vs. extrinsic motives, overcoming procrastination, and adjusting goals to remain both challenging and feasible.


Reflection Model Instructions
Drive Discovery
	INSTRUCTIONS:
	Help learners define meaningful goals, explore underlying motivations, and plan strategies to maintain drive.
	EXAMPLE INTERACTION:
	                                                                                                                                                                                    * Student: "I have goals but lose motivation really fast."
                                                                                                                                                                                    * AI: "Let’s explore why each goal matters. Connecting goals to personal values often boosts perseverance."
	STARTING PROMPT:
	                                                                                                                                                                                    * What’s a goal you’re pursuing, and why is it important to you? What keeps you going when challenges arise?
	RESTRICTIONS
	                                                                                                                                                                                    * Reflect on internal motivators; no guaranteed success or outcomes.
	Grade Level Examples
	Elementary (Grades 1–5):
	                                                                                                                                                                                    * A 3rd grader sets a weekly reading goal out of curiosity about new stories.
                                                                                                                                                                                    * A 5th grader wants to learn an instrument, inspired by a musician role model.
	Middle School (Grades 6–8):
	                                                                                                                                                                                    * A 6th grader aims for better math grades to feel more confident.
                                                                                                                                                                                    * An 8th grader trains regularly to join the school basketball team next season.
	High School (Grades 9–12):
	                                                                                                                                                                                    * A 9th grader sets a fitness target, tracking progress daily.
                                                                                                                                                                                    * A 12th grader remains motivated by envisioning college or career outcomes.
	College – Undergraduate:
	                                                                                                                                                                                    * A freshman identifies long-term career interests, setting interim academic milestones.
                                                                                                                                                                                    * A junior invests energy in a capstone project, aligning it with future job prospects.
	College – Graduate:
	                                                                                                                                                                                    * A master’s student ties thesis work to a personal passion for social impact.
                                                                                                                                                                                    * A doctoral candidate sees their research as potentially transformative, fueling persistence.
	Related Fields
                                                                                                                                                                                    * Goal-Setting in Organizational Psychology: Shows that challenging, specific goals boost performance, with reflection guiding alignment and commitment.
                                                                                                                                                                                    * Educational Goal Theory: Differentiates mastery vs. performance goals, linking them to academic engagement and resilience.
                                                                                                                                                                                    * Sports Psychology: Investigates how goal-setting and motivation regulation support athletic persistence and mental toughness.
                                                                                                                                                                                    * Behavioral Economics: Examines time-inconsistent preferences and how reflecting on immediate vs. long-term rewards can correct procrastination.
Known Theories
(Classic) Goal-Setting Theory (Locke & Latham)
Short Description:
 Goal-Setting Theory posits that specific, challenging goals lead to higher performance if individuals are committed and receive feedback. Reflection ensures alignment between goals, feedback, and personal resources.

(Classic) Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan)
Short Description:
 SDT differentiates intrinsic motivation (driven by interest or enjoyment) from extrinsic motivation (driven by external rewards). Reflective strategies help individuals identify and enhance autonomy, competence, and relatedness in goal pursuit.
