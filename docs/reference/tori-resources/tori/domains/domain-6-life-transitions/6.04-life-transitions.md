6.4 Reflection on Life Transitions
Definition
Reflection on Life Transitions is the introspective focus on how one copes with and finds meaning in significant shifts in life roles or circumstances, whether anticipated or unexpected.
Reflection Model Instructions
Milestone Mind
	INSTRUCTIONS:
	Provide a reflective space for bigger transitions (graduation, career moves, relocating), exploring emotional and practical shifts.
	EXAMPLE INTERACTION:
	                                                                                          * Student: "I’m leaving home for college. I’m excited but terrified."
                                                                                          * AI: "Big transitions often bring mixed emotions. What are you most excited about, and what do you think you’ll miss or worry about?"
	STARTING PROMPT:
	                                                                                          * Which life transition are you undergoing or recently completed, and how do you feel about it?
	RESTRICTIONS
	                                                                                          * Focus on personal reflection about transitions; no official relocation or career guidance.
	Grade Level Examples
	Elementary (Grades 1–5):
	                                                                                          * 	Middle School (Grades 6–8):
	                                                                                          * A 7th grader adapts to a new sibling at home, changing family dynamics.
                                                                                          * An 8th grader finishes middle school, preparing for high school’s different environment.
	High School (Grades 9–12):
	                                                                                          * A 9th grader transitions from homeschool to public school, adjusting to social differences.
                                                                                          * A 12th grader braces for college or the workforce, feeling nostalgic yet eager.
	College – Undergraduate:
	                                                                                          * A freshman experiences dorm life away from family for the first time.
                                                                                          * A senior contemplates job searching or grad school after finishing a degree.
	College – Graduate:
	                                                                                          * A master’s student is near completion, juggling final projects and future job prospects.
                                                                                          * A doctoral candidate completes a dissertation, choosing between academic or non-academic careers.
	

Related Fields

                                                                                          * Sociology of the Life Course

Theories
                                                                                             * Schlossberg’s Transition Theory – Defines transitions as events or nonevents that alter roles and assumptions, influenced by personal and situational factors.

                                                                                             * Endings–Neutral Zone–New Beginnings (Bridges) – Distinguishes external changes from internal transitions, describing a three-phase journey through any major life shift.

                                                                                             * Adult Transition & Learning (Merriam) – Examines how adults learn and develop through transitions, framing them as catalysts for personal growth.

Key References (Newest to Oldest)
                                                                                                1. Anderson, M. L., Goodman, J., & Schlossberg, N. K. (2012). Counseling adults in transition (4th ed.). New York: Springer.

                                                                                                   * Summary: Updates Schlossberg’s 4S model (Situation, Self, Supports, Strategies) for analyzing adult transitions, providing practical applications in counseling and life coaching.

                                                                                                   * Link: https://www.springerpub.com/counseling-adults-in-transition-9780826106362.html

                                                                                                      2. Merriam, S. B. (2005). How adult life transitions foster learning and development. New Directions for Adult and Continuing Education, 108, 3–13.

                                                                                                         * Summary: Argues that major life transitions—both planned and unplanned—can spark meaningful adult learning, offering frameworks for counselors and educators.

                                                                                                         * Link: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/ace.193

                                                                                                            3. Bridges, W. (2004). Transitions: Making sense of life’s changes (2nd ed.). Cambridge, MA: Da Capo Press.

                                                                                                               * Summary: Proposes a three-phase model (Endings, Neutral Zone, New Beginnings) and underscores that the emotional reorientation (transition) is distinct from the external change itself.

                                                                                                               * Link: https://www.hachettebookgroup.com/titles/william-bridges/transitions/9780738209043/

                                                                                                                  4. Schlossberg, N. K. (1981). A model for analyzing human adaptation to transition. The Counseling Psychologist, 9(2), 2–18.

                                                                                                                     * Summary: Lays the groundwork for transition theory by highlighting how personal context, perception, and resources shape responses to life shifts.

                                                                                                                     * Link: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/001100008100900202
