Definition
Reflection on Decision-Making involves examining the cognitive processes, strategies, and influences behind one’s choices, from initial information gathering to final selection. It includes identifying the role of biases, emotions, and heuristics that may shape outcomes, as well as evaluating the effectiveness of decision-making frameworks or models used. By reflecting on these elements, individuals can refine their approach, enhance their judgment, and make more deliberate, informed decisions.
Reflection Model Instructions
Choice Compass
	INSTRUCTIONS:
	Encourage learners to weigh pros/cons, consider consequences, and align decisions with personal or shared values.
	EXAMPLE INTERACTION:
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * Student: "Should I join the sports team or focus on the art club?"
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * AI: "List what you value—enjoyment, skill growth, time. Which aligns more with your goals and resources right now?"
	STARTING PROMPT:
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * What decision are you facing, and which factors or values matter most in making it?
	RESTRICTIONS
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * Focus on reflective decision processes, not definitive “best choice.”
	Grade Level Examples
	Elementary (Grades 1–5):
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * A 2nd grader picks between two weekend activities, weighing fun vs. time/cost.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * A 5th grader chooses an elective based on curiosity and future interests.
	Middle School (Grades 6–8):
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * A 6th grader debates staying up late to finish a project vs. getting enough sleep.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * A 7th grader decides between band or drama by reflecting on enjoyment and commitment.
	High School (Grades 9–12):
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * A 9th grader balances extracurriculars to avoid burnout.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * A 12th grader compares multiple colleges by location, cost, and major availability.
	College – Undergraduate:
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * A freshman contemplates switching majors, reflecting on passion vs. job prospects.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * A junior decides between studying abroad vs. taking an internship.
	College – Graduate:
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * A master’s student considers part-time work vs. full academic load.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * A doctoral candidate weighs academia vs. industry career paths, linking each to personal values.
	Related Fields
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * Behavioral Economics: Explores deviations from rational choice models.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * Neuroscience of Decision-Making: Studies brain circuits (e.g., the orbitofrontal cortex) that evaluate risk and reward.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         * Operations Research: Investigates optimization and decision analysis in complex systems.

Known Theories
a) Prospect Theory (Kahneman, Tversky)
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            * Short Description: By Kahneman and Tversky, explains how people evaluate potential losses and gains with certain cognitive biases (loss aversion, framing effects).

Barberis, N. C. (2018). “Thirty years of prospect theory in economics.” Journal of Economic Perspectives, 32(2).

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * Summary: Reviews the impact of prospect theory on modern economics and finance.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     2. Tversky, A., & Kahneman, D. (1992). “Advances in prospect theory: Cumulative representation of uncertainty.” Journal of Risk and Uncertainty, 5(4).

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * Summary: Extends original prospect theory to account for cumulative probabilities.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           3.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               * Summary: Foundational paper revealing how real-world decision-making deviates from expected utility.

b) Naturalistic Decision-Making (Klein et al.)
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             * Short Description: Focuses on how people make decisions in real-life, high-stakes settings (e.g., firefighting, military), emphasizing experience, intuition, and situational awareness.
