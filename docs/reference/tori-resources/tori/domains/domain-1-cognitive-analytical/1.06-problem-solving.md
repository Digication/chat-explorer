Definition
Reflection on Problem-Solving involves examining the methods, strategies, and thought processes used to address challenges and arrive at solutions. It includes critically reviewing how the problem was defined, what information or resources were considered, and how choices or strategies were evaluated over time. By reflecting on these steps, individuals can refine their approach, improving both the efficiency and creativity of future problem-solving efforts.
Reflection Model Instructions
Solution Steps
	INSTRUCTIONS:
	Help learners define problems clearly, brainstorm possible solutions, and evaluate outcomes.
	EXAMPLE INTERACTION:
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Student: "I can’t organize our group project at all."
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * AI: "First, let’s list each major task, then assign roles based on strengths. Breaking it down can reveal how to proceed step by step."
	STARTING PROMPT:
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * What problem are you facing, and how could you break it into smaller, actionable steps?
	RESTRICTIONS
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Emphasize reflective steps, not providing final solutions.
	Grade Level Examples
	Elementary (Grades 1–5):
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * A 2nd grader tries multiple ways to build a block structure, reflecting on each attempt.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * A 4th grader outlines steps for a science fair project instead of diving in blindly.
	Middle School (Grades 6–8):
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * A 6th grader fixes a simple robotics error using trial and error, taking notes.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * A 7th grader tests multiple ways to research a topic before choosing the best method.
	High School (Grades 9–12):
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * A 9th grader resolves a scheduling conflict by listing possible compromises.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * An 11th grader clarifies roles in a group project to reduce misunderstandings.
	College – Undergraduate:
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * A freshman uses a flowchart approach to tackle a difficult calculus problem.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * A junior manages a campus event, mapping tasks on a timeline and delegating effectively.
	College – Graduate:
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * A master’s student divides a thesis question into smaller sub-questions for manageable study.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * A doctoral candidate refines experimental prototypes through iterative feedback.
	
Related Fields
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Cognitive Psychology: Focuses on heuristics, strategies, and mental sets in solving problems.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Industrial & Organizational Psychology: Applies problem-solving frameworks to workplace challenges.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   * Computer Science (Artificial Intelligence): Studies algorithmic approaches to complex problem-solving.

Known Theories
a) Means-End Analysis (Newell, Simon)
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      * Short Description: A classic problem-solving technique where individuals systematically reduce the difference between the current state and goal state by identifying sub-goals.
