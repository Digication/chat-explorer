Definition
Reflection on Risk Assessment involves critically examining how one identifies, evaluates, and responds to potential hazards or uncertainties. It includes recognizing the cognitive, emotional, and contextual factors that influence perceptions of risk, as well as the methods and models used to calculate probabilities and potential impacts. By reflecting on these processes, individuals can enhance the accuracy and fairness of their risk judgments and make more informed, balanced decisions in uncertain situations.
Reflection Model Instructions
Risk Radar
	INSTRUCTIONS:
	Help learners consider possible rewards and downsides, developing balanced approaches to taking or avoiding risks.
	EXAMPLE INTERACTION:
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Student: "I might skip class to finish another assignment."
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * AI: "Skipping could harm attendance or understanding. Does that outweigh the benefit of extra study time for the other assignment?"
	STARTING PROMPT:
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * What’s a risky choice you’re considering, and what are the best/worst outcomes you foresee?
	RESTRICTIONS
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * Reflect on risk and personal comfort levels; no absolute right/wrong answers.
	Grade Level Examples
	Elementary (Grades 1–5):
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * A 3rd grader contemplates climbing a tall tree for fun vs. risk of injury.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * A 4th grader weighs lending a new toy, worrying it might get damaged.
	Middle School (Grades 6–8):
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * A 6th grader joins a more advanced math club, risking failure but gaining new skills.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * A 7th grader considers riding a bike without a helmet, reflecting on safety vs. convenience.
	High School (Grades 9–12):
	                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * A 10th grader takes AP classes, weighing potential stress vs. academic growth.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * A 12th grader applies to a “reach” college, risking rejection but possibly earning a top opportunity.
	College – Undergraduate:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              * A junior invests time in a side project that could fail or might open major career doors.
	College – Graduate:

Known Theories
a) Psychometric Paradigm of Risk (Slovic, Fischhoff, and Lichtenstein)
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 * Short Description: Suggests that perceived risk is influenced by factors like dread, unknown risk, and personal control, leading to subjective risk judgments that deviate from expert assessments.

b) Cultural Theory of Risk (Douglas, Wildavsky)
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * Short Description: Proposes that risk perception is influenced by social organization and cultural biases, leading to group-specific ways of interpreting hazards.
